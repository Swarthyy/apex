1. Product Overview

1.1 Vision Statement

APEX is a personal intelligence dashboard that aggregates biometric,
behavioural, and lifestyle data into a single interface, surfacing
actionable insights about what genuinely drives an individual's peak
performance. It speaks only when it has something worth saying.

1.2 The Core Problem

High-performing individuals track fragments of their health and
performance across disconnected apps — a sleep tracker here, a gym log
there, a calorie counter somewhere else. None of these tools talk to
each other, none understand context, and none are capable of answering
the question that actually matters:

  "Why do I feel incredible some weeks and average others — and what
  exactly caused it?"

APEX solves this by treating data holistically, correlating inputs
across sectors, and presenting them in a format that rewards engagement
and makes pattern recognition intuitive.

1.3 Design Philosophy

-   Low friction, passive data collection wherever possible.

-   The interface should feel like a dashboard built for a
    high-performance athlete, not a patient.

-   Notifications are scarce and high signal — maximum one per day,
    earned by genuine pattern detection.

-   Dopamine-inducing positive feedback when doing well. Visual rewards
    for consistency.

-   Brutally honest. No sugar-coating trend data.

-   The app knows you. It uses calendar context, historical patterns,
    and cross-sector correlations to give advice that generic apps
    cannot.

1.4 Target User

APEX is built for a single user archetype initially: a health-conscious,
performance-oriented individual who:

-   Actively tracks multiple facets of their health and life.

-   Follows an ancestral or primal nutrition philosophy (raw dairy,
    grass-fed meat, organ consumption).

-   Practises semen retention and understands its correlation with
    performance metrics.

-   Trains regularly and logs gym sessions.

-   Maintains a detailed Google Calendar.

-   Is entrepreneurially driven and understands the value of data-driven
    decision making.

-   Wants a single interface that replaces the five-app friction
    currently required to understand their own patterns.

2.1 Vitality Score

Each day is assigned a composite Vitality Score (1.0–10.0) synthesised
from all active sectors, weighted by their historical correlation with
subjective wellbeing. The score drives the colour-coded Historical
Calendar view and enables long-term pattern analysis.

  ---------------- ------------------------------------------------------
  Formula          Weighted average: Energy (25%) + Mood (20%) + Libido
                   (20%) + Sleep quality (20%) + SR day modifier (15%)

  ---------------- ------------------------------------------------------

The score is displayed prominently throughout the app — as a coloured
cell in the calendar view (deep red → amber → lime green), as the
central metric in the mobile home screen, and as the title metric in
each Historical day detail panel.

3. Screens & Views

3.1 Dashboard (Home)

The primary landing screen. Displays a live snapshot of the current day
across all sectors.

Components

-   Topbar — Logo, tab navigation (Dashboard / Insights / Chat),
    date/time chip, Morning Check-in CTA button.

-   Sidebar — Grouped navigation for all sectors plus Overview and
    Settings. Colour-coded with sector dot indicators. Active state with
    coloured right border.

-   Stat Tile Row — Five primary tiles (SR Streak, Sleep, Energy,
    Weight, Gym). Each tile has: coloured gradient background, blurred
    glow orb, metric value, sub-label, animated bottom progress bar,
    hover glow effect, click-through to sector focus view.

-   Active Insights Row — Four insight cards with type-coded left
    borders (positive / warning / info / alert). Each card is clickable
    and navigates to the relevant sector.

-   Trends Graph — 30-day energy vs libido dual-line SVG chart with
    gradient fill, toggle for 7D/30D/90D timeframes, and SR streak day
    annotation.

-   SR Card — Large standalone retention counter with personal best
    comparison and streak start date.

-   Right Panel — Fixed notifications feed, Quick Ask chat interface,
    and integration status badges.

3.3 Nutrition Feed

A standalone screen accessible from the Nutrition sector and from the
top navigation. Designed as a dopamine-inducing vertical timeline feed —
not a clinical calorie tracker.

Day Navigation

-   Left/right arrow navigation to swipe through previous days with a
    smooth slide-in animation.

-   Day header showing date, "Today" / "Yesterday" / "N days ago"
    sub-label.

-   Sticky daily totals bar showing calorie progress bar, macro pills
    (kcal / protein / fat / carbs), and a Nutrition Score (1–10).

The Feed Timeline

Meals are rendered as cards on a vertical timeline with a coloured
dot-and-line connector:

-   Each meal card has: meal type tag (Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner /
    Snack / Supplement, colour coded), meal name, calorie count, an
    RPG-style stats panel (mini bars for calories/protein/fat/carbs),
    and a nutrient bonus chip row.

-   Nutrient bonus chips (green "+") appear dynamically based on what
    was logged: +High Protein, +Vitamin A, +B12, +Raw Enzymes, +CLA,
    +Zinc, +Omega-3, +Choline, +Fat-soluble Vitamins, +Vitamin K2.

-   A pulsing red NOW marker sits at the current time within the feed.
    Meals above it are past; the upcoming recommendation is below it.

-   An expandable Ingredients Breakdown section appears on each card.

Upcoming Recommendation

Below the NOW marker, a dashed teal card displays APEX's recommended
next meal. It considers:

-   Remaining calorie budget for the day.

-   SR day — higher SR day = higher protein and zinc priority.

-   Calendar — if a gym session is scheduled, protein target increases.

-   Current nutrient gaps (e.g., no liver logged this week → recommend
    organ meat).

Right Panel — Daily Summary

-   Animated score ring (SVG, gradient fill) showing the day's Nutrition
    Score.

-   Full nutrient breakdown with individual bars (Vitamin A, B12, Zinc,
    Iron, etc.).

-   7-day calorie bar chart showing the week at a glance.

-   Today's Wins — achievement list that populates as goals are met
    (e.g., "Liver logged", "Zinc maxed", "Protein on track").

Log Modal

Triggered by the "+ Log Meal" button in the topbar. Fields: Meal type,
Food name/description, Calories, Protein, Fat, Carbs, Photo upload
(optional). Submitted meals animate into the feed and update all totals
instantly.

3.4 Historical Calendar View

A dedicated screen for long-term pattern analysis. Accessible from the
top navigation (History tab).

Calendar Modes

-   Month View — Standard calendar grid (Mon–Sun), each cell coloured by
    Vitality Score. Cells show the day number, score badge, and event
    dots (orange = gym, pink = social, gold = work). Hover lifts cell.
    Click opens Day Detail panel.

-   Week View — Four-week band showing daily score cells at larger size
    with the same colour system.

-   Year View — GitHub-style heatmap: all 12 months rendered as 31-cell
    rows, allowing instant identification of high and low periods across
    the year.

Day Detail Panel (right side)

Clicking any day opens a rich breakdown in the right panel:

-   Date heading + Vitality Score (large, colour-coded).

-   Metrics table: Energy, Mood, Libido, Sleep, Weight, SR Day, Gym,
    Liver, Raw Milk, Oysters.

-   Calendar Events: Synced Google Calendar entries for that day with
    time and colour-coded event type.

-   APEX Analysis: A written analysis (2–4 sentences) explaining why the
    day scored as it did, with supporting green "+positive" chips and
    amber "warning" chips.

Month Averages Sparklines

Below the calendar grid, a row of horizontal bar charts shows monthly
averages for Vitality, Energy, Mood, Libido, Sleep, and Gym Days —
enabling fast month-to-month comparison.

3.5 Insights View

A consolidated list of all APEX-generated insights with full text,
confidence level, and contributing sectors. Each insight card shows its
detection timestamp and the data sources that generated it.

3.6 Chat / Ask APEX

A conversational interface backed by the full personal data store. The
user can ask natural language questions and receive data-grounded
answers.

Example queries

-   "What was I eating when I felt best last month?"

-   "Why has my libido been lower this week?"

-   "What does my best week look like in data?"

-   "Should I go to the gym today given my sleep?"

-   "How does my mood correlate with my SR streak?"

3.7 Mobile View

A phone-optimised layout accessible from the desktop dashboard via the

Mobile layout differences from desktop

-   Hero card replaces the stat tile grid — shows SR streak as the

-   Horizontal scroll for sector cards — swipe through Sleep, Energy,

-   Morning check-in CTA is a prominent full-width button.

-   Rounded card styling (12–16px radius) throughout — native mobile

-   Mini-graph card showing the 14-day energy trend.

-   Nutrition quick-card with calorie bar and nutrient bonus chips.

4. Intelligence Layer

4.1 Insight Generation

APEX runs a nightly pattern detection job across all stored data.
Insight generation logic:

-   Cross-sector correlation detection — identifies when two or more
    sectors diverge from their norm simultaneously.

-   SR streak milestone detection — at days 7, 10, 14, 21, 30, triggers
    a performance window notification.

-   Nutritional gap detection — if a high-correlation food (liver, raw
    milk, oysters) hasn't been logged in N days, surfaces a warning with
    the last-seen correlation data.

-   Sleep debt accumulation — 3 consecutive nights under threshold
    triggers a recovery recommendation, cross-referenced with upcoming
    calendar whitespace.

-   Calendar context integration — knows what's in the user's Google
    Calendar and uses it to personalise recommendations (e.g., "you have
    a rest day tomorrow — ideal sleep window").

-   Recomp trend detection — if weight is declining while gym volume is
    increasing over a 14+ day window, flags lean mass gain.

4.2 Notification Rules

APEX enforces strict notification discipline to avoid alert fatigue:

-   Maximum 1 push notification per day.

-   Notifications are tiered: Performance (SR milestones, trend peaks) →
    Warnings (nutritional gaps, sleep debt) → Reminders (morning
    check-in).

-   Morning check-in prompt delivered at a user-configured time (default
    8am).

-   Notifications are suppressed if the user has already completed their
    check-in.

4.3 Chat Memory Bank

The APEX Chat interface queries a structured memory bank built from all
ingested data:

-   Full historical log of every sector for every day.

-   Calendar events with timestamps and categories.

-   Nutrition logs including specific foods and associated nutrient
    bonuses.

-   Detected patterns and previously surfaced insights.

The AI layer (Claude / GPT-4o) is given this context at query time and
generates grounded, specific answers — not generic health advice.

5.2 Data Layer

All data is persisted in Supabase (PostgreSQL). Core tables:

-   daily_checkins — morning/evening scores for energy, mood, libido, SR
    day.

-   sleep_logs — duration, bedtime, wake time, quality score (synced
    from Apple Health / Oura).

-   gym_sessions — session date, exercise list, total volume, PR flags
    (synced from Hevy).

-   body_metrics — weight, body fat %, muscle mass (synced from
    Withings).

-   nutrition_logs — per-meal entries with photo URL, macros, food name,
    meal type, nutrient flags.

-   calendar_events — synced Google Calendar entries with event name,
    time, duration, auto-categorised (work/gym/social/other).

-   insights — generated insight records with timestamp, type,
    confidence, sector tags.

5.4 AI Pipeline

-   Nightly batch job: runs correlation analysis across all sectors,
    generates insight candidates, filters by confidence threshold,
    writes top candidate to insights table.

-   Chat: user query → relevant data context assembled from Supabase →
    sent to Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 with structured prompt → response
    streamed to UI.

-   Nutrition photo AI: photo uploaded → sent to GPT-4o Vision with
    prompt "estimate macros for this meal" → returns structured JSON →
    pre-fills log modal.

aesthetic intentionally evokes performance dashboards — F1 telemetry,

Typography

-   Display / Headings: Syne (Google Fonts) — 400, 600, 700, 800
    weights. Used for sector titles, metric values, score numbers, card
    headings.

-   Body / Monospace: DM Mono — 300, 400, 500 weights. Used for all
    labels, tags, timestamps, data values, body copy. Reinforces the
    data/technical aesthetic.

Spacing & Layout

-   Desktop: 3-column grid — 220px sidebar / flexible main / 300px right
    panel. 58px topbar.

-   Mobile: Full-bleed single column with 16px horizontal padding. 58px
    bottom nav bar.

-   Card padding: 16–20px. Gap between cards: 10–14px.

Motion

-   Stat bar animations: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1), 1.2s duration.
    Triggered on view load.

-   View transitions: fadeUp — opacity 0→1, translateY 8px→0, 0.3s ease.

-   Meal cards: slideIn — opacity 0→1, translateX -12px→0, staggered
    80ms per card.

-   Bonus chips: popIn — scale 0.8→1, 0.3s ease, staggered 50ms per
    chip.

-   Phone shell modal: translateY 20px→0, cubic-bezier spring bounce,
    0.35s.

8.1 Success Metrics

APEX will be considered successful at the end of Phase 1 if:

-   Daily check-in completion rate exceeds 80% over a 30-day period.

-   At least 3 cross-sector correlations are detected and verified by
    the user as accurate.

-   The Nutrition Feed is used to log at least 2 meals per day on
    average.

-   The Historical Calendar shows a visible pattern between SR streak
    days and high Vitality Score weeks.

At Phase 3 completion:

-   APEX Chat can accurately answer 5 of 5 test queries about historical
    patterns.

-   Insight accuracy (user-rated as correct) exceeds 70%.

-   Nutrition photo AI macro estimation is within 20% of manual entry on
    80% of meals.

9. Out of Scope (v1)

The following features are explicitly excluded from the initial build to
maintain focus:

-   Multi-user support — APEX v1 is a single-user personal tool.

-   Social features — no public profiles, leaderboards, or community
    sharing.

-   Wearable companion app — APEX reads from Apple Health and Oura via
    their APIs but does not replace these apps.

-   Automated financial data sync — finances remain manual entry only.

-   Prescription or medical advice — APEX provides pattern observations,
    not clinical recommendations.

-   Paid subscription model — the app is personal infrastructure, not a
    commercial product in v1.
