1. WHAT IS APEX
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

APEX is a personal intelligence dashboard — an iOS mobile app and web-based
desktop application — that pulls together all of the data a health-conscious,
performance-focused individual already generates across separate apps, devices,
and manual logs, and presents it in one unified interface.

It is available on:
  - iOS (native mobile app, React Native via Expo)
  - Web browser — desktop (Next.js web app)

The mobile app is the primary daily-use surface. The desktop web app is the
deep analysis and review surface. Both share the same backend and data store.
They are two views of the same system, not two separate products.

APEX is not:
  - A fitness app
  - A calorie counter
  - A mood journal
  - A habit tracker

It is the layer above all of those things. It reads from them, correlates them,
and tells you what they mean together.

Tagline: APEX — The dashboard for your life.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. THE PROBLEM IT SOLVES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A performance-oriented person in 2026 generates data constantly:

  - Sleep tracked in Apple Health or Oura
  - Workouts logged in Hevy
  - Weight and body composition synced from Withings
  - Nutrition half-tracked in a notes app or not at all
  - Mood and energy self-assessed but never written down
  - Calendar events sitting in Google Calendar with no connection to anything

None of these tools talk to each other. None of them can answer the question
that actually matters:

  "Why did I feel incredible that week in January — and how do I replicate it?"

Every app only sees its own slice of the picture. APEX holds the whole picture
and finds the patterns across it. It does not replace any existing app. It sits
above them, reading from all of them, and synthesises their data into something
a human can actually use.

3. VISION & DIRECTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CORE VALUE PROPOSITION
  APEX connects all your data silos into one dashboard, then surfaces the
  patterns and correlations that explain your performance — automatically.

WHO IT IS FOR (initially)
  The first users of APEX are Jett and a small close circle of friends who
  share a similar approach to health and performance: ancestral nutrition,
  semen retention practice, active gym training, and a genuine desire to
  understand the data behind how they feel. This is not a mass-market wellness
  app. The initial user is someone who would build this themselves if they had
  the time.

PRODUCT TRAJECTORY
  Phase 1 — Build it for yourself. Get it working, log real data, validate
             that the insight engine is accurate.

  Phase 2 — Give it to close friends. Onboard a small circle, see if it works
             for their data patterns too, gather feedback.

  Phase 3 — Productise. If it is genuinely useful for others, build the
             subscription or freemium commercial layer and release it properly.

  Business model (when applicable): Monthly subscription or freemium (free
  basic tier + paid for AI insights and full history). Decision deferred until
  the product has proven itself with real users.

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
  - Low friction. Passive data collection wherever possible. The user should
    not feel like they are working to feed the system.
  - Dopamine-inducing. When you are doing well, the app makes it feel good.
    Green scores, visual streaks, RPG-style nutrient bonuses, rewarding
    animations.
  - Speaks when it has something to say. Maximum one notification per day.
    APEX earns the right to notify by being high-signal.
  - Brutally honest. No smoothing of bad data. If your week was poor, the
    calendar is red and the scores reflect it.
  - Contextually aware. APEX knows what is in your calendar. It uses that
    knowledge to make better recommendations.
  - Built for a high-performance athlete, not a patient. The aesthetic,
    language, and data density reflect that.

5. CORE DATA SECTORS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

APEX organises all tracked data into ten sectors. Each sector has its own
colour identity, data source, and insight logic. All sectors feed into the
daily Vitality Score.

  SECTOR          COLOUR          PRIMARY METRIC          DATA SOURCE         INPUT TYPE
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  SR / Retention  Lime green      Streak days             Manual              Passive
  Sleep           Electric blue   Duration + quality      Apple Health/Oura   Passive
  Energy          Gold            Morning score (1-10)    Daily check-in      Active (30s)
  Gym             Orange          Volume & sessions       Hevy API            Passive
  Libido          Pink            Daily score (1-10)      Daily check-in      Active (30s)
  Nutrition       Teal            Calories + nutrients    Photo log + manual  Active (2 min)
  Body Comp       Purple          Weight + body fat %     Withings API        Passive
  Mood            Amber           Daily score (1-10)      Daily check-in      Active (30s)
  Finances        Teal            Monthly net cash flow   Manual              Active (weekly)
  Business        Blue            Focus score + projects  Manual + Calendar   Active (daily)

PASSIVE SECTORS (auto-sync once integrations are live)
  Sleep, Gym, Body Comp — these require zero user action after setup.

ACTIVE SECTORS (require user input)
  Energy, Libido, Mood — captured in the 2-minute morning check-in.
  Nutrition — logged via the Nutrition Feed throughout the day.
  SR — reset button tapped manually when a streak ends.
  Finances, Business — reviewed and updated weekly.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. THE VITALITY SCORE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Every day is assigned a Vitality Score between 1.0 and 10.0. This is the
single composite number that represents how good a day was across all sectors.

FORMULA (v1, to be validated with real data)
  Energy score        25%
  Mood score          20%
  Libido score        20%
  Sleep quality       20%
  SR day modifier     15%

The SR modifier is additive — higher streak day = higher score ceiling.
Sleep quality is derived from duration (sub-6h penalised, 8h+ rewarded).

WHERE IT APPEARS
  - Colour of each cell in the Historical Calendar (red to amber to lime green)
  - Central metric on the mobile home screen
  - Day detail panel in the Historical View
  - Month averages sparklines

COLOUR SCALE
  1-2   Deep red        (#500808 to #7a1010)
  3-4   Red-orange      (#c03020 to #d06018)
  5-6   Amber           (#e09020 to #f0c040)
  7-8   Green           (#52b030 to #7ec825)
  9-10  Lime green      (#a8e020 to #c8f135)

7. DAILY HABIT LOOP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

APEX is designed around a mixed interaction model:

MORNING (2 minutes — mobile)
  1. Push notification arrives at configured time (default 8am).
  2. User opens app, completes Morning Check-in:
       - Energy on waking (1-10 slider)
       - Mood (1-10 slider)
       - Libido (1-10 slider)
       - SR day (auto-increments, manual reset button)
       - Optional: notes / journal line
  3. Dashboard updates with today's scores.
  4. APEX surfaces one insight if a pattern has been detected overnight.

THROUGHOUT THE DAY (passive)
  - Hevy syncs workout data automatically after each session.
  - Withings syncs weight each morning after weigh-in.
  - Apple Health / Oura syncs sleep data each morning.
  - Google Calendar events are ingested for context.

MEAL LOGGING (active, throughout day — mobile)
  - User taps "+ Log Meal" in the Nutrition Feed.
  - Types meal name, macros (or uploads a photo for AI estimation).
  - Feed updates with the new card, nutrient bonuses, and calorie progress.

OCCASIONAL DEEP DIVE (desktop — typically weekly)
  - Open desktop web app.
  - Review Historical Calendar for the week/month.
  - Browse Insights view for newly detected patterns.
  - Use APEX Chat to ask specific questions about recent data.
  - Review sector focus views for any that flagged in the week's insights.

8.1 DASHBOARD (HOME)
────────────────────

The primary landing screen. Shows a live snapshot of today across all sectors.

Desktop layout:
  - Topbar: Logo | Tab nav (Dashboard / Insights / Chat / History / Nutrition)
            | Date chip | Morning Check-in CTA button
  - Sidebar: Grouped nav for all 10 sectors + Overview and Settings sections.
             Colour-coded dot per sector. Active item has coloured right border.
  - Main content area: Stat tiles, insights cards, trends graph, SR card.
  - Right panel: Notifications feed, Quick Ask chat, integration status.

STAT TILE ROW (5 tiles)
  Each tile shows: sector label, primary metric value, sub-label, animated
  bottom progress bar. Hover state lifts the card with a coloured glow shadow.
  Click navigates to that sector's focus view.
  Tiles: SR Streak | Sleep | Energy | Weight | Gym

ACTIVE INSIGHTS ROW (2x2 grid)
  Four insight cards. Type-coded by left border colour:
    Positive  — lime green border (good pattern detected)
    Warning   — orange border (gap or risk detected)
    Info      — blue border (neutral observation)
    Alert     — pink border (anomaly or concern)
  Each card is clickable and navigates to the relevant sector.

TRENDS GRAPH
  30-day dual-line chart: Energy vs Libido, with gradient fill under each line.
  Timeframe toggles: 7D / 30D / 90D.
  SR streak day annotations appear as vertical dashed markers.

SR CARD
  Standalone lime green card showing the current streak count (large), personal
  best, and streak start date.

RIGHT PANEL
  Notifications: Colour-tagged alerts (max shown: 5 most recent).
  Quick Ask: Compact chat input with one prior exchange shown.
  Integrations: Live/Connected status badges for each data source.

8.3 NUTRITION FEED
───────────────────

A standalone screen accessible from the top nav (Nutrition tab) and from the
Nutrition sector focus view. Designed as a dopamine-inducing vertical timeline
feed — not a clinical calorie tracker.

DAY NAVIGATION
  Left/right arrows to move between days. Smooth slide animation on transition.
  Header shows: date, "Today" / "Yesterday" / "N days ago".
  Sticky totals bar below header: calorie progress bar (gradient fill),
  macro pills (kcal / protein / fat / carbs), Nutrition Score (1-10).

THE FEED TIMELINE
  Meals appear as cards on a vertical timeline with coloured dot connectors.

  Each meal card contains:
    - Meal type tag: Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner / Snack / Supplement
      (each colour-coded: gold / teal / purple / orange / blue)
    - Meal name and description
    - Calorie count (large, lime green)
    - RPG stat panel: four mini bars for kcal / protein / fat / carbs,
      each with a label, value, and proportional fill bar
    - Nutrient bonus chips (green "+" chips, animate in on load):
        +High Protein    +Vitamin A       +B12
        +Raw Enzymes     +CLA             +Zinc
        +Omega-3         +Choline         +Fat-soluble Vitamins
        +Vitamin K2      +Sat. Fat (neutral)   +High Carb (warning, orange)
    - Expandable ingredients breakdown (tap to reveal)
    - Photo thumbnail if a photo was uploaded with the log

  NOW MARKER
    A pulsing red dot marks the current time within the feed. Meals logged
    before now appear above it. The upcoming recommendation appears below it.

  UPCOMING RECOMMENDATION CARD
    Dashed teal border card below the NOW marker.
    Powered by: remaining calorie budget + SR day + today's calendar events
    + current nutrient gaps.
    Shows: recommended meal name, reason (1-2 sentences), target macro chips.
    Example: "SR day 14 + gym tonight — high protein + zinc priority.
              You need ~960 more kcal to hit goal."

RIGHT PANEL — DAILY SUMMARY
  - Animated SVG score ring showing the day's Nutrition Score.
  - Full nutrient breakdown bars: Calories, Protein, Fat, Carbs, Vitamin A,
    B12, Zinc, Iron. Bars turn lime green and show a tick when goal is met.
  - 7-day calorie bar chart for the week.
  - Today's Wins: achievement list that populates as nutritional goals are hit.
    Examples: "Liver logged — B12 maxed", "Oysters logged — Zinc 127% RDA",
              "Protein on track — 148g / 200g goal".

LOG MODAL
  Triggered by "+ Log Meal" button. Fields:
    Meal type (dropdown)
    Food name / description (text input)
    Calories (number)
    Protein (number)
    Fat (number)
    Carbs (number)
    Photo upload (optional — triggers AI macro estimation in Phase 3)
  On submit: new card animates into the feed, totals update, bonus chips pop in.


8.4 HISTORICAL CALENDAR
────────────────────────

Dedicated screen for long-term pattern analysis. Accessible from the History
tab in top navigation.

CALENDAR MODES (toggle between three)

  MONTH VIEW
    Standard Mon-Sun calendar grid. Each day cell is coloured by Vitality Score
    using the colour scale defined in Section 6. Each cell shows:
      - Day number (top left)
      - Vitality Score badge (bottom right)
      - Up to 3 event dots (bottom left):
          Orange dot = gym session
          Pink dot   = social event
          Gold dot   = work shift
    Hover: cell lifts and gains a glow border. Click: opens Day Detail panel.
    Today's cell has a white border.

  WEEK VIEW
    A 4-week band. Taller cells showing the same colour coding and dots.
    Useful for comparing individual days across recent weeks.

  YEAR VIEW
    GitHub-style heatmap. 12 rows (one per month), 31 columns (one per day).
    Small cells coloured by Vitality Score. Instant full-year pattern visibility.
    Clicking a cell jumps to that month in Month View and opens Day Detail.

DAY DETAIL PANEL (right side)
  Opens when any day cell is clicked. Contains:
    - Date and day name
    - Vitality Score (large, colour-coded to score)
    - Metrics table:
        Energy / Mood / Libido / Sleep / Weight / SR Day /
        Gym (Yes/Rest) / Liver (logged/not) / Raw Milk / Oysters
    - Calendar Events: Google Calendar entries for that day, with time
      and colour-coded event type (work / gym / social / other)
    - APEX Analysis: 2-4 sentence written explanation of why the day
      scored as it did. Supported by green chip (+positive factors) and
      orange chips (warning factors / what was missing).

MONTH AVERAGES SPARKLINES
  Below the calendar grid, horizontal bar charts showing monthly averages for:
  Vitality, Energy, Mood, Libido, Sleep avg, Gym days.
  Enables instant month-on-month comparison at a glance.

8.6 CHAT / ASK APEX
────────────────────

A conversational interface backed by the full personal data store.
The user types natural language questions and receives grounded, specific
answers — not generic health advice.

The AI has access to:
  - All historical sector data
  - All nutrition logs
  - All calendar events
  - All previously detected insights

Example questions APEX can answer:
  "What was I eating when I felt best last month?"
  "Why has my libido been lower this week?"
  "What does my best week look like in data?"
  "Should I go to the gym today given my sleep?"
  "How does my mood track with my SR streak?"
  "What's the longest I've gone without logging liver?"

The chat interface on desktop is a full-screen view. On mobile it is the "Ask"
tab in the bottom navigation. Both use the same backend AI endpoint.

MVP note: For Phase 1, chat uses keyword-matching canned responses against
demo data. Full AI (Claude claude-sonnet-4-6) is wired in Phase 3 when real data exists.

9. INTELLIGENCE LAYER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.1 NIGHTLY INSIGHT JOB
  Runs once per day across all stored data. Produces a ranked list of insight
  candidates. Only the highest-confidence candidate is surfaced as a
  notification the following morning.

DETECTION LOGIC

  SR correlation analysis
    Quantifies the relationship between SR streak day and all other sectors
    (energy, libido, gym volume, mood). Detects peaks and dip windows.
    Fires insight at days 7, 10, 14, 21, 30.

  Nutritional gap detection
    Tracks consumption frequency of high-correlation foods: liver, raw milk,
    oysters, grass-fed beef. If any drops below user-set frequency threshold,
    surfaces a warning with the last correlation data (e.g., "Last time you
    logged liver 3x/week, your libido averaged 1.8 points higher").

  Sleep debt accumulation
    Counts consecutive nights below 7.5h threshold. If 3+ nights, triggers
    recovery recommendation. Cross-references Google Calendar for upcoming
    free time and includes it in the recommendation.

  Recomp detection
    If weight trend is down AND gym volume trend is up over 14+ days,
    flags lean mass gain pattern.

  Calendar context
    Scans upcoming calendar for gym sessions, social events, work blocks.
    Uses this to personalise the meal recommendation in the Nutrition Feed
    and to time insights appropriately.

  General cross-sector anomaly
    When two or more sectors diverge from their 30-day baseline simultaneously,
    flags for investigation.

9.2 NOTIFICATION RULES
  - Maximum 1 push notification per day.
  - Priority queue: Performance alerts > Nutritional warnings > Reminders.
  - Suppressed if morning check-in is already complete.
  - Never sent between 10pm and 7am.

9.3 CHAT AI PIPELINE
  User query
  > Relevant data assembled from Supabase (last 90 days of all sectors,
    relevant nutrition logs, calendar events, prior insights)
  > Sent to Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 with structured system prompt
  > Response streamed back to UI

9.4 NUTRITION PHOTO AI (Phase 3)
  Photo uploaded in log modal
  > Sent to GPT-4o Vision with prompt: "Estimate macros for this meal.
    Return JSON: {kcal, protein_g, fat_g, carbs_g, confidence}"
  > JSON pre-fills the log modal fields
  > User confirms / adjusts before submitting

10.2 MVP IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

The existing 4 HTML prototypes are unified into a single Vite-based SPA:

  src/styles.css   Consolidated design system from all 4 prototypes.
                   All CSS variables, shared component styles, responsive
                   rules, mobile shell, nutrition feed, calendar.

  src/app.js       Main application logic:
                     Router / View Manager (go() function)
                     Demo Data Store (DEMO_DATA object, pre-seeded)
                     Check-in Modal (energy/mood/libido sliders + SR day)
                     Meal Log Modal (writes to demo data, updates feed)
                     Chat Engine (keyword-match canned responses for MVP)
                     Desktop to Mobile Toggle
                     Graph Toggle Buttons (7D/30D/90D)
                     Stat Bar Animations
                     Calendar Engine (month/week/year, Vitality colour coding)
                     Nutrition Feed Engine (timeline, NOW marker, meal cards)

  index.html       Single entry point, all views unified here.

  Views unified from all prototypes:
    Dashboard, 10 sector views, Insights, Chat, Integrations,
    History (calendar), Nutrition Feed, Mobile Overlay.

10.3 DATABASE SCHEMA (Supabase)

  daily_checkins       morning/evening scores: energy, mood, libido, SR day,
                       optional notes. One row per user per day.

  sleep_logs           duration, bedtime, wake time, quality score.
                       Synced from Apple Health / Oura.

  gym_sessions         session date, exercises, total volume, PR flags.
                       Synced from Hevy.

  body_metrics         weight, body fat %, muscle mass, measurement date.
                       Synced from Withings.

  nutrition_logs       meal entries: photo URL, macros (kcal/protein/fat/carbs),
                       food name, meal type, nutrient bonus flags, timestamp.

  calendar_events      event name, start/end time, auto-category
                       (work/gym/social/health/other), source: Google Calendar.

  insights             generated insight records: text, type, confidence,
                       sector tags, timestamp, read/unread status.

10.4 AUTHENTICATION
  Supabase Auth. Email + password for v1. Apple Sign-In for iOS in Phase 2.
  Row Level Security (RLS) on all tables — each user sees only their own data.

10.5 DATA SYNC STRATEGY
  Passive integrations run on a scheduled Supabase Edge Function (nightly for
  Withings and Apple Health export, near-real-time webhook for Hevy).
  Google Calendar syncs on app open and every 6 hours.

15. OPEN QUESTIONS & DECISIONS NEEDED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  1. SUBSCRIPTION VS FREEMIUM MODEL
     Both viable. Defer until Phase 4 when real users exist to test willingness
     to pay. Lean toward freemium: free tier covers manual entry, paid tier
     unlocks AI insights, API integrations, and full history.

  2. SR DAY RESET — BUTTON TAP OR INFERRED?
     Button tap is simplest and most reliable for v1. Inference from
     journal/check-in data possible in later phases.

  3. EVENING CHECK-IN — REQUIRED OR OPTIONAL?
     Optional to avoid friction. Could capture end-of-day mood, libido, and
     a journal line. Prompt via notification at user-configured time (default 9pm).

  4. VITALITY SCORE WEIGHTING — VALIDATED?
     Current formula: Energy 25% + Mood 20% + Libido 20% + Sleep 20% + SR
     modifier 15%. Needs real data to validate. Should be user-adjustable in
     settings eventually.

  5. GOOGLE CALENDAR EVENT CATEGORISATION — AUTO OR MANUAL?
     Keyword auto-matching for v1 (e.g., "gym", "work", "dinner" in event
     title maps to category). Manual override available. Full ML categorisation
     in Phase 3.

  6. AI PROVIDER FOR CHAT?
     Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 preferred for instruction-following and context handling.
     GPT-4o Vision used separately for nutrition photo estimation only.

  7. HOW ARE INVITED FRIENDS ONBOARDED?
     Invite link via email. They create their own account, configure their own
     integrations, have completely separate private data. No shared data in v1.

  8. OFFLINE SUPPORT ON MOBILE?
     Morning check-in should work offline and sync when connection is restored.
     Charts and insights require a connection. Decision: local SQLite cache for
     check-in data as a safety net.
